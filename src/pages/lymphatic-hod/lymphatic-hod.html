Hodgkin's Disease

Hodgkin's Disease (sometimes referred to as HD) attacks the same area that Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma does: the lymphocytes. Hodgkin's Disease takes over these cells, and in doing so weakens the immune system.

This has the adverse effect of making the body more prone to sickness. Because of this, the symptoms of Hodgkin's Disease are very similar to Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma.

One of the most important factors in ensuring that Hodgkin's Disease is dealt with effectively is the stage at which the cancer is discovered.

This can be seen through analyzing the relative survival rates by stage that those who contract the disease have historically faced. While the prognosis for the first 2 stages is relatively positive, with 90 percent of patients surviving 5 years or more, only 65 percent of patients whose cancer was discovered in stage IV achieved this same milestone.

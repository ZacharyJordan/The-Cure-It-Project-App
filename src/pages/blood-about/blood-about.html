About Blood Cancer

Blood Cancers are diseases in which normal blood cell development is disturbed by the unchecked growth of a varient on an ordinary blood cell. There are 3 primary forms of Blood Cancer: Leukemia, Lymphoma, and Myeloma.

We will get into the specifics of each later in the Blood Cancer page. First, let us take a moment to acknowledge the problem as it currently exists.

Blood Cancers are among the more promient types of Cancer, accounting for nearly 10 percent of the deaths associated with Cancer in 2014. To provide a sense of context for these figures, consider the fact that someone in the United States is diagnosed with a blood Cancer every 4 minutes, and that deaths from Blood Cancer occur approximately once every 10 minutes.
